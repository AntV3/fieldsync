## Primary post

You don't find out a job is underwater until the money's already gone.

Every project manager I talk to has the same quiet problem: by the time what's happening in the field reaches the office, it's weeks old. The foreman knows Level 3 wrapped up Tuesday. The office is still billing off last week's spreadsheet. The change order happened verbally on site and got written down… never.

That gap is exactly where margin disappears — not on the big stuff, but 2% at a time.

So we built FieldSync.

The idea is almost too simple: give the crew one tap. An area is either Working or Done — no "65% complete" guesswork. That status flows to the office in real time and is tied directly to contract value, so what you see on the dashboard is what you can actually bill.

Under that simple surface it does the heavy lifting GCs and subs actually need:

→ Progress that turns straight into draw requests and defensible billing — no reconciliation
→ T&M tickets with photos, crew hours, and signatures captured on site
→ Change order requests tracked and exported to clean PDFs — nothing slips through
→ Earned value (CPI/SPI, forecasts) so you see a slip before it costs you
→ Works on a phone in the field, online or off

Foremen actually use it because an update takes under 15 seconds. The office finally sees the job as it really is — today, not next month.

## Posting tips

- **First line is everything.** LinkedIn truncates after ~2 lines; the hook decides whether anyone expands it. Both versions front-load the pain.
- **Post the link in the first comment, not the body.** LinkedIn suppresses reach on posts with outbound links. Put the demo/site link as your own first comment.
- **Best times:** Tue–Thu, 7–9am or 12–1pm in your customers' time zone.
- **Reply fast.** Answer every "pilot"/comment within the first hour — early engagement drives the algorithm and turns comments into conversations.
- **Add one visual.** A 10–15s screen recording of the one-tap field update → dashboard updating, or a single clean dashboard screenshot, will roughly double engagement over text-only.
- **Tag sparingly.** If a pilot customer or partner is open to it, tagging 1–2 relevant people beats 10 hashtags.
